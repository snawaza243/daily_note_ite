A Complete Guide to Eid al-Adha (Bakra Eid) Salah: Step-by-Step with Arabic, English, and Hindi Transliteration

Introduction

Eid al-Adha Salah, also known as Bakra Eid prayer, is a special congregational prayer performed on the morning of Eid al-Adha, the Festival of Sacrifice. It commemorates Prophet Ibrahim’s (AS) willingness to sacrifice his son in obedience to Allah’s command. The Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) said, "The first thing we do on the day of Eid is to offer the prayer" (Sahih Bukhari). This prayer consists of two Rak’ahs with additional Takbirs, followed by a Khutbah, and is often followed by the Qurbani (sacrifice). This guide details the two Rak’ahs of Eid al-Adha Salah, including Arabic recitations, their transliterations in English and Hindi, and translations, along with guidance on Qurbani preparation. Whether you’re new to Eid al-Adha Salah or seeking to perfect your prayer, this blog will help you perform it with devotion.

Understanding Eid al-Adha Salah

Eid al-Adha Salah is performed in congregation, typically in an open area or mosque, shortly after sunrise on the 10th of Dhul-Hijjah. It consists of two Rak’ahs with seven additional Takbirs in the first Rak’ah and five in the second, recited aloud by the Imam. The Khutbah follows, offering reminders about sacrifice and devotion. The Quran states, "So pray to your Lord and sacrifice [to Him alone]" (Surah Al-Kawthar, 108:2). Unlike Eid al-Fitr, no food is eaten before the prayer, and Zakat al-Fitr is not required. Instead, those who can afford it perform Qurbani (sacrifice of an animal, typically a goat, sheep, cow, or camel) after the prayer, sharing the meat with family, friends, and the poor. To prepare, perform Ghusl, wear clean or new clothes, apply perfume (for men), and recite Takbirat loudly en route to the prayer ground. Men, women, and children are encouraged to attend, fostering unity.

Step-by-Step Guide to Eid al-Adha Salah

Eid al-Adha Salah consists of two Rak’ahs with additional Takbirs, prayed in congregation and led by the Imam. Below is the detailed process for each Rak’ah, with recitations in Arabic, English transliteration, Hindi transliteration, and translations.

Preparation for Eid al-Adha Salah

Note: Before heading to the prayer, perform Ghusl, wear clean or new clothes, and apply perfume (for men). Unlike Eid al-Fitr, do not eat before the prayer. Recite the Takbirat (Allahu Akbar, Allahu Akbar, La ilaha illallah, Allahu Akbar, Allahu Akbar, wa lillahil hamd) loudly while going to the prayer ground. Arrange for Qurbani if applicable.

اللّٰهُ أَكْبَرُ، اللّٰهُ أَكْبَرُ، لَا إِلٰهَ إِلَّا اللّٰهُ، اللّٰهُ أَكْبَرُ، اللّٰهُ أَكْبَرُ، وَلِلّٰهِ الْحَمْدُ

English: Allahu Akbar, Allahu Akbar, La ilaha illallah, Allahu Akbar, Allahu Akbar, wa lillahil hamd

Hindi: अल्लाहु अकबर, अल्लाहु अकबर, ला इलाहा इल्लल्लाह, अल्लाहु अकबर, अल्लाहु अकबर, व लिल्लाहिल हम्द

Translation: Allah is the Greatest, Allah is the Greatest, there is no god but Allah, Allah is the Greatest, Allah is the Greatest, and to Allah belongs all praise.

First Rak’ah

1. Niyyah (Intention)

Make a silent intention in your heart for two Rak’ahs of Eid al-Adha Salah.

نَوَيْتُ أَنْ أُصَلِّيَ لِلّٰهِ تَعَالَىٰ رَكْعَتَيْ صَلَاةِ عِيدِ الْأَضْحَىٰ

English: Nawaytu an usalli lillahi ta’ala rak’atay salatil ‘eidil adha

Hindi: नवायतु अन उस्सल्ली लिल्लाही तआला रकअतय सलातिल ईदिल अज़हा

Translation: I intend to offer two Rak’ahs of Eid al-Adha prayer for Allah, the Exalted.

2. Takbiratul Ihram (Opening Takbir)

Follow the Imam, raising hands to ear level and saying “Allahu Akbar” to begin the prayer.

اللّٰهُ أَكْبَرُ

English: Allahu Akbar

Hindi: अल्लाहु अकबर

Translation: Allah is the Greatest.

3. Additional Takbirs (Seven Takbirs)

Follow the Imam, raising hands and saying “Allahu Akbar” seven times before recitation. Hands are folded after each Takbir (Hanafi school) or left down (other schools). No specific dua is recited between Takbirs, though silent Dhikr is permissible.

اللّٰهُ أَكْبَرُ

English: Allahu Akbar (x7)

Hindi: अल्लाहु अकबर (सात बार)

Translation: Allah is the Greatest (repeated seven times).

4. Qiyam (Standing) - Recite Surah Al-Fatiha

Listen to the Imam recite Surah Al-Fatiha and another Surah (often Surah Al-A’la) aloud. Say “Ameen” silently after Al-Fatiha.

بِسْمِ ٱللَّهِ ٱلرَّحْمَٰنِ ٱلرَّحِيمِ
ٱلْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ رَبِّ ٱلْعَٰلَمِينَ
ٱلرَّحْمَٰنِ ٱلرَّحِيمِ
مَٰلِكِ يَوْمِ ٱلدِّينِ
إِيَّاكَ نَعْبُدُ وَإِيَّاكَ نَسْتَعِينُ
ٱهْدِنَا ٱلصِّرَٰطَ ٱلْمُسْتَقِيمَ
صِرَٰطَ ٱلَّذِينَ أَنْعَمْتَ عَلَيْهِمْ غَيْرِ ٱلْمَغْضُوبِ عَلَيْهِمْ وَلَا ٱلضَّآلِّينَ

English:
Bismillahir Rahmanir Rahim
Alhamdu lillahi Rabbil ‘alamin
Ar-Rahmanir Rahim
Maliki yawmiddin
Iyyaka na’budu wa iyyaka nasta’in
Ihdinas siratal mustaqim
Siratal ladhina an’amta ‘alayhim ghayril magdubi ‘alayhim walad dallin

Hindi:
बिस्मिल्लाहिर रहमानिर रहीम
अलहमदु लिल्लाही रब्बिल आलमीन
अर-रहमानिर रहीम
मालिकी यौमिद्दीन
इय्या नअबुदु व इय्या नस्तईन
इहदिनस सिरातल मुस्तकीम
सिरातल लज़ीना अनअमता अलयहिम गैरिल मगदूबि अलयहिम वलद दाल्लीन

Translation:
In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful.
All praise is due to Allah, Lord of all the worlds.
The Most Gracious, the Most Merciful.
Master of the Day of Judgment.
You alone we worship, and You alone we ask for help.
Guide us to the straight path,
The path of those upon whom You have bestowed favor, not of those who have earned Your anger, nor of those who are astray.

5. Ruku (Bowing)

Follow the Imam into Ruku, saying “Subhana Rabbiyal Adheem” three times silently.

سُبْحَانَ رَبِّيَ الْعَظِيمِ

English: Subhana Rabbiyal Adheem

Hindi: सुभान रब्बियल अज़ीम

Translation: Glory be to my Lord, the Most Great.

6. Qawmah (Standing after Ruku)

Stand with the Imam, saying silently:

سَمِعَ اللّٰهُ لِمَنْ حَمِدَهُ، رَبَّنَا وَلَكَ الْحَمْدُ

English: Sami’Allahu liman hamidah, Rabbana wa lakal hamd

Hindi: समिअल्लाहु लिमन हमिदह, रब्बना व लकल हम्द

Translation: Allah hears those who praise Him; Our Lord, to You be all praise.

7. Sujood (Prostration)

Prostrate with the Imam, saying “Subhana Rabbiyal A’la” three times silently.

سُبْحَانَ رَبِّيَ الْأَعْلَىٰ

English: Subhana Rabbiyal A’la

Hindi: सुभान रब्बियल आला

Translation: Glory be to my Lord, the Most High.

8. Jalsa (Sitting between Prostrations)

Sit briefly with the Imam, saying silently:

رَبِّ اغْفِرْ لِي

English: Rabbighfir li

Hindi: रब्बिगफिर ली

Translation: My Lord, forgive me.

9. Second Sujood

Repeat the prostration with the Imam, saying “Subhana Rabbiyal A’la” three times silently (same as step 7).

Second Rak’ah

10. Qiyam (Standing)

Stand with the Imam for the second Rak’ah.

11. Additional Takbirs (Five Takbirs)

Follow the Imam, raising hands and saying “Allahu Akbar” five times before recitation. Hands are folded after each Takbir (Hanafi school) or left down (other schools).

اللّٰهُ أَكْبَرُ

English: Allahu Akbar (x5)

Hindi: अल्लाहु अकबर (पाँच बार)

Translation: Allah is the Greatest (repeated five times).

12. Qiyam - Recite Surah Al-Fatiha

Listen to the Imam recite Surah Al-Fatiha and another Surah (often Surah Al-Ghashiyah) aloud. Say “Ameen” silently after Al-Fatiha.

13. Ruku, Qawmah, Sujood, Jalsa, Second Sujood

Follow the Imam through Ruku, Qawmah, Sujood, Jalsa, and second Sujood, reciting the same supplications as in the first Rak’ah (steps 5-9).

14. Tashahhud (Sitting)

Sit with the Imam and recite silently:

التَّحِيَّاتُ لِلّٰهِ وَالصَّلَوَاتُ وَالطَّيِّبَاتُ، السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكَ أَيُّهَا النَّبِيُّ وَرَحْمَةُ اللّٰهِ وَبَرَكَاتُهُ، السَّلَامُ عَلَيْنَا وَعَلَىٰ عِبَادِ اللّٰهِ الصَّالِحِينَ، أَشْهَدُ أَنْ لَا إِلٰهَ إِلَّا اللّٰهُ وَأَشْهَدُ أَنَّ مُحَمَّدًا عَبْدُهُ وَرَسُولُهُ

English:
At-tahiyyatu lillahi was-salawatu wat-tayyibat, Assalamu ‘alaika ayyuhan-Nabiyyu wa rahmatullahi wa barakatuh, Assalamu ‘alaina wa ‘ala ‘ibadillahis-salihin, Ash-hadu an la ilaha illallah, wa ash-hadu anna Muhammadan ‘abduhu wa rasuluh

Hindi:
अत-तहिय्यातु लिल्लाही वस-सलवातु वत-तय्यिबात, अस्सलामु अलैका अय्युहन-नबिय्यु व रहमतुल्लाही व बरकातुह, अस्सलामु अलैना व अला इबादिल्लाहिस-सालिहीन, अश-हदु अन ला इलाहा इल्लल्लाह, व अश-हदु अन्न मुहम्मदन अब्दुहु व रसूलुह

Translation:
All greetings, prayers, and good things are for Allah. Peace be upon you, O Prophet, and the mercy and blessings of Allah. Peace be upon us and upon the righteous servants of Allah. I bear witness that there is no god but Allah, and I bear witness that Muhammad is His servant and Messenger.

15. Salat Ibrahimiyyah (Durood)

Recite silently:

اللّٰهُمَّ صَلِّ عَلَىٰ مُحَمَّدٍ وَعَلَىٰ آلِ مُحَمَّدٍ كَمَا صَلَّيْتَ عَلَىٰ إِبْرَاهِيمَ وَعَلَىٰ آلِ إِبْرَاهِيمَ إِنَّكَ حَمِيدٌ مَجِيدٌ، اللّٰهُمَّ بَارِكْ عَلَىٰ مُحَمَّدٍ وَعَلَىٰ آلِ مُحَمَّدٍ كَمَا بَارَكْتَ عَلَىٰ إِبْرَاهِيمَ وَعَلَىٰ آلِ إِبْرَاهِيمَ إِنَّكَ حَمِيدٌ مَجِيدٌ

English:
Allahumma salli ‘ala Muhammadin wa ‘ala ali Muhammadin kama sallayta ‘ala Ibrahima wa ‘ala ali Ibrahima innaka Hamidun Majid. Allahumma barik ‘ala Muhammadin wa ‘ala ali Muhammadin kama barakta ‘ala Ibrahima wa ‘ala ali Ibrahima innaka Hamidun Majid.

Hindi:
अल्लाहुम्मा सल्ली अला मुहम्मदिन व अला आलि मुहम्मदिन कामा सल्लयता अला इब्राहीमा व अला आलि इब्राहीमा इन्नका हमीदुन मजीद। अल्लाहुम्मा बारिक अला मुहम्मदिन व अला आलि मुहम्मदिन कामा बारकता अला इब्राहीमा व अला आलि इब्राहीमा इन्नका हमीदुन मजीद।

Translation:
O Allah, send blessings upon Muhammad and upon the family of Muhammad as You sent blessings upon Ibrahim and upon the family of Ibrahim; indeed, You are Praiseworthy, Glorious. O Allah, bless Muhammad and the family of Muhammad as You blessed Ibrahim and the family of Ibrahim; indeed, You are Praiseworthy, Glorious.

16. Dua After Durood

Recite a short dua silently, such as:

رَبَّنَا آتِنَا فِي الدُّنْيَا حَسَنَةً وَفِي الْآخِرَةِ حَسَنَةً وَقِنَا عَذَابَ النَّارِ

English: Rabbana atina fid-dunya hasanatan wa fil-akhirati hasanatan wa qina ‘adhaban-nar

Hindi: रब्बना आतिना फिद-दुन्या हसनतन व फिल-आखिरति हसनतन व किना अज़ाबन-नार

Translation: Our Lord, give us good in this world and good in the Hereafter, and protect us from the punishment of the Fire.

17. Salam (Ending the Prayer)

Follow the Imam, turning your head to the right and left, saying silently:

السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُمْ وَرَحْمَةُ اللّٰهِ

English: Assalamu ‘alaikum wa rahmatullah

Hindi: अस्सलामु अलैकुम व रहमतुल्लाह

Translation: Peace and mercy of Allah be upon you.

Khutbah (Sermon)

Note: After the prayer, the Imam delivers a Khutbah in two parts, typically in Arabic followed by the local language. It includes praise of Allah, blessings on the Prophet (PBUH), and reminders about sacrifice and devotion. Listening attentively is recommended, though not obligatory as in Jumu'ah.

Qurbani (Sacrifice)

Note: After the Salah and Khutbah, those who can afford it perform Qurbani, sacrificing an animal (e.g., goat, sheep, cow, camel) in remembrance of Prophet Ibrahim’s (AS) obedience. The meat is divided into three parts: one for the family, one for friends/relatives, and one for the poor. Ensure the animal is healthy and the slaughter is performed according to Islamic guidelines, preferably after the Eid Salah and before the 12th of Dhul-Hijjah.

Intention for Qurbani:

نَوَيْتُ أَنْ أُضَحِّيَ لِلّٰهِ تَعَالَىٰ

English: Nawaytu an udhahhiya lillahi ta’ala

Hindi: नवायतु अन उज़ह्हिया लिल्लाही तआला

Translation: I intend to perform the sacrifice for Allah, the Exalted.

Post-Prayer Practices

Note: After Eid Salah, greet others with “Eid Mubarak,” exchange embraces, and share meals. Proceed with Qurbani if applicable, and distribute the meat generously. Continue reciting Takbirat throughout the days of Tashriq (11th to 13th Dhul-Hijjah). No additional Rak’ahs are required after Eid Salah.

Tips for Perfecting Eid al-Adha Salah

Eid al-Adha Salah is a profound act of worship that honors sacrifice and obedience. Here are some tips:

  • Preparation: Perform Ghusl, wear your best clothes, and recite Takbirat loudly en route to the prayer ground. Arrange Qurbani logistics in advance.
  • Takbirs: Practice the additional Takbirs (seven in the first Rak’ah, five in the second) to follow the Imam smoothly. Say “Allahu Akbar” clearly.
  • Spiritual Motivation: Reflect on the Hadith: "The son of Adam does no deed on the day of sacrifice more beloved to Allah than shedding blood [of Qurbani]" (Sunan Tirmidhi).
  • Avoid Mistakes: Follow the Imam closely, maintain proper rows, and avoid distractions during the Khutbah.
  • Qurbani and Charity: Ensure the animal is slaughtered humanely, and distribute meat to the poor promptly to maximize the reward.

Performing Eid al-Adha Salah and Qurbani with sincerity deepens your faith and community ties. Seek Allah’s acceptance for your worship.

Conclusion

Eid al-Adha Salah, or Bakra Eid prayer, is a sacred celebration of sacrifice and devotion. With two Rak’ahs featuring additional Takbirs, a Khutbah, and the Qurbani, it unites Muslims in gratitude and obedience.
