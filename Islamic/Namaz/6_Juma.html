A Complete Guide to Jumu'ah Salah: Step-by-Step with Arabic, English, and Hindi Transliteration

Introduction

Jumu'ah Salah, the Friday congregational prayer, is a special obligation for Muslim men, replacing the Dhuhr prayer on Fridays. It’s a time for community unity, reflection, and listening to the Khutbah (sermon). The Quran says, "O you who have believed, when the call is made for prayer on the day of Jumu'ah, then proceed to the remembrance of Allah and leave trade" (Surah Al-Jumu'ah, 62:9). The Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) emphasized its importance: "The best day on which the sun rises is Friday" (Sahih Muslim). This guide details the four Rak’ahs Sunnah Mu’akkadah before, two Rak’ahs Fard, and four Rak’ahs Sunnah after Jumu'ah Salah, including Arabic recitations, their transliterations in English and Hindi, and translations. Whether you’re new to Jumu'ah or seeking to perfect your prayer, this blog will help you perform it with devotion.

Understanding Jumu'ah Salah

Jumu'ah Salah is performed in congregation at a mosque after the sun passes its zenith, typically around midday. It consists of four Rak’ahs Sunnah Mu’akkadah (highly recommended) before the Khutbah, two Rak’ahs Fard (obligatory) led by the Imam, and four Rak’ahs Sunnah Mu’akkadah after. The Khutbah, delivered in two parts, is an integral part of Jumu'ah, replacing the two Rak’ahs of Dhuhr’s Fard. The Fard Rak’ahs are recited aloud by the Imam, while Sunnah Rak’ahs are prayed silently and individually. To prepare, perform Wudu, wear clean clothes, apply perfume (for men), and arrive early to listen attentively to the Khutbah. Jumu'ah is obligatory for adult Muslim men in good health; women may attend but can pray Dhuhr at home instead.

Step-by-Step Guide to Jumu'ah Salah

Jumu'ah Salah includes four Rak’ahs Sunnah Mu’akkadah before the Khutbah, two Rak’ahs Fard during the congregational prayer, and four Rak’ahs Sunnah Mu’akkadah after. Below is the detailed process for each Rak’ah, with recitations in Arabic, English transliteration, Hindi transliteration, and translations.

Sunnah Mu’akkadah Rak’ahs Before Khutbah (4 Rak’ahs)

Note: These Sunnah Rak’ahs are prayed in two sets of two Rak’ahs, individually and silently, before the Khutbah begins. They are highly recommended to earn extra rewards.

First Set: First Rak’ah

1. Niyyah (Intention)

Make a silent intention in your heart for four Rak’ahs of Jumu'ah Sunnah Mu’akkadah (first set of two).

نَوَيْتُ أَنْ أُصَلِّيَ لِلّٰهِ تَعَالَىٰ رَكْعَتَيْ صَلَاةِ الْجُمُعَةِ سُنَّةً مُؤَكَّدَةً

English: Nawaytu an usalli lillahi ta’ala rak’atay salatil jumu’ah sunnatan mu’akkadatan

Hindi: नवायतु अन उस्सल्ली लिल्लाही तआला रकअतय सलातिल जुमुआह सुन्नतन मुआक्कदतन

Translation: I intend to offer two Rak’ahs of Jumu'ah Sunnah Mu’akkadah prayer for Allah, the Exalted.

2. Takbiratul Ihram (Opening Takbir)

Raise your hands to ear level, say “Allahu Akbar,” and begin the prayer.

اللّٰهُ أَكْبَرُ

English: Allahu Akbar

Hindi: अल्लाहु अकबर

Translation: Allah is the Greatest.

3. Qiyam (Standing) - Recite Surah Al-Fatiha

Recite Surah Al-Fatiha silently, followed by a short Surah (e.g., Surah Al-Ikhlas).

بِسْمِ ٱللَّهِ ٱلرَّحْمَٰنِ ٱلرَّحِيمِ
ٱلْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ رَبِّ ٱلْعَٰلَمِينَ
ٱلرَّحْمَٰنِ ٱلرَّحِيمِ
مَٰلِكِ يَوْمِ ٱلدِّينِ
إِيَّاكَ نَعْبُدُ وَإِيَّاكَ نَسْتَعِينُ
ٱهْدِنَا ٱلصِّرَٰطَ ٱلْمُسْتَقِيمَ
صِرَٰطَ ٱلَّذِينَ أَنْعَمْتَ عَلَيْهِمْ غَيْرِ ٱلْمَغْضُوبِ عَلَيْهِمْ وَلَا ٱلضَّآلِّينَ

English:
Bismillahir Rahmanir Rahim
Alhamdu lillahi Rabbil ‘alamin
Ar-Rahmanir Rahim
Maliki yawmiddin
Iyyaka na’budu wa iyyaka nasta’in
Ihdinas siratal mustaqim
Siratal ladhina an’amta ‘alayhim ghayril magdubi ‘alayhim walad dallin

Hindi:
बिस्मिल्लाहिर रहमानिर रहीम
अलहमदु लिल्लाही रब्बिल आलमीन
अर-रहमानिर रहीम
मालिकी यौमिद्दीन
इय्या नअबुदु व इय्या नस्तईन
इहदिनस सिरातल मुस्तकीम
सिरातल लज़ीना अनअमता अलयहिम गैरिल मगदूबि अलयहिम वलद दाल्लीन

Translation:
In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful.
All praise is due to Allah, Lord of all the worlds.
The Most Gracious, the Most Merciful.
Master of the Day of Judgment.
You alone we worship, and You alone we ask for help.
Guide us to the straight path,
The path of those upon whom You have bestowed favor, not of those who have earned Your anger, nor of those who are astray.

4. Ruku (Bowing)

Bow and say “Subhana Rabbiyal Adheem” three times.

سُبْحَانَ رَبِّيَ الْعَظِيمِ

English: Subhana Rabbiyal Adheem

Hindi: सुभान रब्बियल अज़ीम

Translation: Glory be to my Lord, the Most Great.

5. Qawmah (Standing after Ruku)

Stand up and say:

سَمِعَ اللّٰهُ لِمَنْ حَمِدَهُ، رَبَّنَا وَلَكَ الْحَمْدُ

English: Sami’Allahu liman hamidah, Rabbana wa lakal hamd

Hindi: समिअल्लाहु लिमन हमिदह, रब्बना व लकल हम्द

Translation: Allah hears those who praise Him; Our Lord, to You be all praise.

6. Sujood (Prostration)

Prostrate and say “Subhana Rabbiyal A’la” three times.

سُبْحَانَ رَبِّيَ الْأَعْلَىٰ

English: Subhana Rabbiyal A’la

Hindi: सुभान रब्बियल आला

Translation: Glory be to my Lord, the Most High.

7. Jalsa (Sitting between Prostrations)

Sit briefly and say:

رَبِّ اغْفِرْ لِي

English: Rabbighfir li

Hindi: रब्बिगफिर ली

Translation: My Lord, forgive me.

8. Second Sujood

Repeat the prostration and say “Subhana Rabbiyal A’la” three times (same as step 6).

First Set: Second Rak’ah

9. Qiyam (Standing)

Stand up and repeat steps 3-8 (recite Surah Al-Fatiha and another Surah, then perform Ruku, Qawmah, Sujood, Jalsa, and second Sujood).

10. Tashahhud (Sitting)

Sit and recite:

التَّحِيَّاتُ لِلّٰهِ وَالصَّلَوَاتُ وَالطَّيِّبَاتُ، السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكَ أَيُّهَا النَّبِيُّ وَرَحْمَةُ اللّٰهِ وَبَرَكَاتُهُ، السَّلَامُ عَلَيْنَا وَعَلَىٰ عِبَادِ اللّٰهِ الصَّالِحِينَ، أَشْهَدُ أَنْ لَا إِلٰهَ إِلَّا اللّٰهُ وَأَشْهَدُ أَنَّ مُحَمَّدًا عَبْدُهُ وَرَسُولُهُ

English:
At-tahiyyatu lillahi was-salawatu wat-tayyibat, Assalamu ‘alaika ayyuhan-Nabiyyu wa rahmatullahi wa barakatuh, Assalamu ‘alaina wa ‘ala ‘ibadillahis-salihin, Ash-hadu an la ilaha illallah, wa ash-hadu anna Muhammadan ‘abduhu wa rasuluh

Hindi:
अत-तहिय्यातु लिल्लाही वस-सलवातु वत-तय्यिबात, अस्सलामु अलैका अय्युहन-नबिय्यु व रहमतुल्लाही व बरकातुह, अस्सलामु अलैना व अला इबादिल्लाहिस-सालिहीन, अश-हदु अन ला इलाहा इल्लल्लाह, व अश-हदु अन्न मुहम्मदन अब्दुहु व रसूलुह

Translation:
All greetings, prayers, and good things are for Allah. Peace be upon you, O Prophet, and the mercy and blessings of Allah. Peace be upon us and upon the righteous servants of Allah. I bear witness that there is no god but Allah, and I bear witness that Muhammad is His servant and Messenger.

11. Salat Ibrahimiyyah (Durood)

Recite:

اللّٰهُمَّ صَلِّ عَلَىٰ مُحَمَّدٍ وَعَلَىٰ آلِ مُحَمَّدٍ كَمَا صَلَّيْتَ عَلَىٰ إِبْرَاهِيمَ وَعَلَىٰ آلِ إِبْرَاهِيمَ إِنَّكَ حَمِيدٌ مَجِيدٌ، اللّٰهُمَّ بَارِكْ عَلَىٰ مُحَمَّدٍ وَعَلَىٰ آلِ مُحَمَّدٍ كَمَا بَارَكْتَ عَلَىٰ إِبْرَاهِيمَ وَعَلَىٰ آلِ إِبْرَاهِيمَ إِنَّكَ حَمِيدٌ مَجِيدٌ

English:
Allahumma salli ‘ala Muhammadin wa ‘ala ali Muhammadin kama sallayta ‘ala Ibrahima wa ‘ala ali Ibrahima innaka Hamidun Majid. Allahumma barik ‘ala Muhammadin wa ‘ala ali Muhammadin kama barakta ‘ala Ibrahima wa ‘ala ali Ibrahima innaka Hamidun Majid.

Hindi:
अल्लाहुम्मा सल्ली अला मुहम्मदिन व अला आलि मुहम्मदिन कामा सल्लयता अला इब्राहीमा व अला आलि इब्राहीमा इन्नका हमीदुन मजीद। अल्लाहुम्मा बारिक अला मुहम्मदिन व अला आलि मुहम्मदिन कामा बारकता अला इब्राहीमा व अला आलि इब्राहीमा इन्नका हमीदुन मजीद।

Translation:
O Allah, send blessings upon Muhammad and upon the family of Muhammad as You sent blessings upon Ibrahim and upon the family of Ibrahim; indeed, You are Praiseworthy, Glorious. O Allah, bless Muhammad and the family of Muhammad as You blessed Ibrahim and the family of Ibrahim; indeed, You are Praiseworthy, Glorious.

12. Dua After Durood

Recite a short dua, such as:

رَبَّنَا آتِنَا فِي الدُّنْيَا حَسَنَةً وَفِي الْآخِرَةِ حَسَنَةً وَقِنَا عَذَابَ النَّارِ

English: Rabbana atina fid-dunya hasanatan wa fil-akhirati hasanatan wa qina ‘adhaban-nar

Hindi: रब्बना आतिना फिद-दुन्या हसनतन व फिल-आखिरति हसनतन व किना अज़ाबन-नार

Translation: Our Lord, give us good in this world and good in the Hereafter, and protect us from the punishment of the Fire.

13. Salam (Ending the Prayer)

Turn your head to the right and left, saying:

السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُمْ وَرَحْمَةُ اللّٰهِ

English: Assalamu ‘alaikum wa rahmatullah

Hindi: अस्सलामु अलैकुम व रहमतुल्लाह

Translation: Peace and mercy of Allah be upon you.

Second Set: Two Rak’ahs

Repeat the steps above for another two Rak’ahs with the same intention.

Khutbah (Sermon)

Note: The Khutbah is delivered by the Imam in two parts, typically in Arabic followed by the local language. Listen attentively, avoid talking, and maintain silence. The Khutbah replaces two Rak’ahs of Dhuhr’s Fard and is an essential part of Jumu'ah.

Fard Rak’ahs (2 Rak’ahs)

Note: The Fard Rak’ahs are prayed in congregation, led by the Imam, with recitations (Surah Al-Fatiha and another Surah) performed aloud. Follow the Imam’s movements and recitations silently.

نَوَيْتُ أَنْ أُصَلِّيَ لِلّٰهِ تَعَالَىٰ رَكْعَتَيْ صَلَاةِ الْجُمُعَةِ فَرْضًا

English: Nawaytu an usalli lillahi ta’ala rak’atay salatil jumu’ah fardan

Hindi: नवायतु अन उस्सल्ली लिल्लाही तआला रकअतय सलातिल जुमुआह फर्दन

Translation: I intend to offer two Rak’ahs of Jumu'ah Fard prayer for Allah, the Exalted.

First Rak’ah

1. Takbiratul Ihram

Follow the Imam, raising hands and saying “Allahu Akbar” silently to begin the prayer.

اللّٰهُ أَكْبَرُ

English: Allahu Akbar

Hindi: अल्लाहु अकबर

Translation: Allah is the Greatest.

2. Qiyam (Standing)

Listen to the Imam recite Surah Al-Fatiha and another Surah aloud. Say “Ameen” silently after Al-Fatiha.

3. Ruku (Bowing)

Follow the Imam into Ruku, saying “Subhana Rabbiyal Adheem” three times silently.

سُبْحَانَ رَبِّيَ الْعَظِيمِ

English: Subhana Rabbiyal Adheem

Hindi: सुभान रब्बियल अज़ीम

Translation: Glory be to my Lord, the Most Great.

Stand with the Imam, saying silently:

English: Sami’Allahu liman hamidah, Rabbana wa lakal hamd

Translation: Allah hears those who praise Him; Our Lord, to You be all praise.

5. Sujood (Prostration)

Prostrate with the Imam, saying “Subhana Rabbiyal A’la” three times silently.

English: Subhana Rabbiyal A’la

Hindi: सुभान रब्बियल आला

English: Rabbighfir li

Hindi: रब्बिगफिर ली

Translation: My Lord, forgive me.

7. Second Sujood

Repeat the prostration with the Imam, saying “Subhana Rabbiyal A’la” three times silently (same as step 5).

Second Rak’ah

8. Qiyam (Standing)

Stand with the Imam, listening to Surah Al-Fatiha and another Surah recited aloud. Say “Ameen” silently after Al-Fatiha. Repeat steps 3-7 (Ruku, Qawmah, Sujood, Jalsa, and second Sujood).

9. Tashahhud (Sitting)

Sit with the Imam and recite silently:

Note: These Sunnah Rak’ahs are prayed in two sets of two Rak’ahs, individually and silently, after the Fard prayer. They can be prayed at the mosque or home, though the mosque is preferred.

Tips for Perfecting Jumu'ah Salah

Jumu'ah Salah is a weekly gathering that strengthens community and faith. Here are some tips:

  • Preparation: Perform Ghusl (full ritual bath), wear clean clothes, and arrive early to secure a spot and pray Sunnah Rak’ahs.
  • Khutbah Etiquette: Listen attentively, avoid talking or distractions, and sit quietly even during pauses.
  • Spiritual Motivation: Reflect on the Hadith: "Whoever performs Ghusl on Friday and goes to Jumu'ah will have their sins forgiven until the next Friday" (Sahih Bukhari).
  • Avoid Mistakes: Follow the Imam closely, maintain proper rows, and use transliterations for accurate pronunciation.
  • After Jumu'ah: Recite Surah Al-Kahf, engage in Dhikr, and make Dua for blessings throughout the week.

Attending Jumu'ah consistently brings immense rewards. Seek Allah’s help to make it a priority.

Conclusion

Jumu'ah Salah is a blessed obligation that unites Muslims weekly. With four Sunnah Rak’ahs before, two Fard Rak’ahs in congregation, and four Sunnah Rak’ahs after, it’s a comprehensive act of worship.
