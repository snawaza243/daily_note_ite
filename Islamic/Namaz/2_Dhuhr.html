A Complete Guide to Dhuhr Salah: Step-by-Step with Arabic, English, and Hindi Transliteration

Introduction

Dhuhr Salah, the midday prayer, is the second of the five daily prayers in Islam, performed after the sun begins to decline from its zenith. It’s a time to pause, reflect, and seek Allah’s guidance amidst the day’s activities. The Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) said, "Whoever prays the five daily prayers will have their sins forgiven" (Sahih Bukhari). This guide details the four Rak’ahs of Sunnah Mu’akkadah, four Rak’ahs of Fard, and two Rak’ahs of Sunnah for Dhuhr Salah, including Arabic recitations, their transliterations in English and Hindi, and translations. Whether you’re new to Salah or seeking to perfect your prayer, this blog is designed to help you perform Dhuhr with confidence and devotion.

Understanding Dhuhr Salah

Dhuhr Salah is performed after the sun passes its zenith until just before Asr time begins. It consists of four Rak’ahs Sunnah Mu’akkadah (highly recommended), four Rak’ahs Fard (obligatory), and two Rak’ahs Sunnah. The Quran emphasizes the importance of Salah: "Guard strictly your prayers, especially the middle prayer" (Surah Al-Baqarah, 2:238), often interpreted as Dhuhr. The Sunnah Rak’ahs are prayed individually and silently, while the Fard Rak’ahs are silent when prayed alone or with the first two Rak’ahs recited aloud in congregation led by an Imam. To prepare, perform Wudu, face the Qibla, and set a clear intention. To stay consistent, designate a quiet space and time for Dhuhr, especially during busy workdays.

Step-by-Step Guide to Dhuhr Salah

Dhuhr Salah consists of four Rak’ahs Sunnah Mu’akkadah, four Rak’ahs Fard, and two Rak’ahs Sunnah. Below is the detailed process for each Rak’ah, with recitations in Arabic, English transliteration, Hindi transliteration, and translations.

Sunnah Mu’akkadah Rak’ahs (4 Rak’ahs)

Note: The Sunnah Mu’akkadah Rak’ahs are prayed in two sets of two Rak’ahs, individually and silently, similar to Fajr Sunnah but with a different intention.

First Set: First Rak’ah

1. Niyyah (Intention)

Make a silent intention in your heart for four Rak’ahs of Dhuhr Sunnah Mu’akkadah (first set of two).

نَوَيْتُ أَنْ أُصَلِّيَ لِلّٰهِ تَعَالَىٰ رَكْعَتَيْ صَلَاةِ الظُّهْرِ سُنَّةً مُؤَكَّدَةً

English: Nawaytu an usalli lillahi ta’ala rak’atay salatidh dhuhr sunnatan mu’akkadatan

Hindi: नवायतु अन उस्सल्ली लिल्लाही तआला रकअतय सलातिझ झुहर सुन्नतन मुआक्कदतन

Translation: I intend to offer two Rak’ahs of Dhuhr Sunnah Mu’akkadah prayer for Allah, the Exalted.

2. Takbiratul Ihram (Opening Takbir)

Raise your hands to ear level, say “Allahu Akbar,” and begin the prayer.

اللّٰهُ أَكْبَرُ

English: Allahu Akbar

Hindi: अल्लाहु अकबर

Translation: Allah is the Greatest.

3. Qiyam (Standing) - Recite Surah Al-Fatiha

Recite Surah Al-Fatiha silently, followed by a short Surah (e.g., Surah Al-Ikhlas).

بِسْمِ ٱللَّهِ ٱلرَّحْمَٰنِ ٱلرَّحِيمِ
ٱلْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ رَبِّ ٱلْعَٰلَمِينَ
ٱلرَّحْمَٰنِ ٱلرَّحِيمِ
مَٰلِكِ يَوْمِ ٱلدِّينِ
إِيَّاكَ نَعْبُدُ وَإِيَّاكَ نَسْتَعِينُ
ٱهْدِنَا ٱلصِّرَٰطَ ٱلْمُسْتَقِيمَ
صِرَٰطَ ٱلَّذِينَ أَنْعَمْتَ عَلَيْهِمْ غَيْرِ ٱلْمَغْضُوبِ عَلَيْهِمْ وَلَا ٱلضَّآلِّينَ

English:
Bismillahir Rahmanir Rahim
Alhamdu lillahi Rabbil ‘alamin
Ar-Rahmanir Rahim
Maliki yawmiddin
Iyyaka na’budu wa iyyaka nasta’in
Ihdinas siratal mustaqim
Siratal ladhina an’amta ‘alayhim ghayril magdubi ‘alayhim walad dallin

Hindi:
बिस्मिल्लाहिर रहमानिर रहीम
अलहमदु लिल्लाही रब्बिल आलमीन
अर-रहमानिर रहीम
मालिकी यौमिद्दीन
इय्या नअबुदु व इय्या नस्तईन
इहदिनस सिरातल मुस्तकीम
सिरातल लज़ीना अनअमता अलयहिम गैरिल मगदूबि अलयहिम वलद दाल्लीन

Translation:
In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful.
All praise is due to Allah, Lord of all the worlds.
The Most Gracious, the Most Merciful.
Master of the Day of Judgment.
You alone we worship, and You alone we ask for help.
Guide us to the straight path,
The path of those upon whom You have bestowed favor, not of those who have earned Your anger, nor of those who are astray.

4. Ruku (Bowing)

Bow and say “Subhana Rabbiyal Adheem” three times.

سُبْحَانَ رَبِّيَ الْعَظِيمِ

English: Subhana Rabbiyal Adheem

Hindi: सुभान रब्बियल अज़ीम

Translation: Glory be to my Lord, the Most Great.

5. Qawmah (Standing after Ruku)

Stand up and say:

سَمِعَ اللّٰهُ لِمَنْ حَمِدَهُ، رَبَّنَا وَلَكَ الْحَمْدُ

English: Sami’Allahu liman hamidah, Rabbana wa lakal hamd

Hindi: समिअल्लाहु लिमन हमिदह, रब्बना व लकल हम्द

Translation: Allah hears those who praise Him; Our Lord, to You be all praise.

6. Sujood (Prostration)

Prostrate and say “Subhana Rabbiyal A’la” three times.

سُبْحَانَ رَبِّيَ الْأَعْلَىٰ

English: Subhana Rabbiyal A’la

Hindi: सुभान रब्बियल आला

Translation: Glory be to my Lord, the Most High.

7. Jalsa (Sitting between Prostrations)

Sit briefly and say:

رَبِّ اغْفِرْ لِي

English: Rabbighfir li

Hindi: रब्बिगफिर ली

Translation: My Lord, forgive me.

8. Second Sujood

Repeat the prostration and say “Subhana Rabbiyal A’la” three times (same as step 6).

First Set: Second Rak’ah

9. Qiyam (Standing)

Stand up and repeat steps 3-8 (recite Surah Al-Fatiha and another Surah, then perform Ruku, Qawmah, Sujood, Jalsa, and second Sujood).

10. Tashahhud (Sitting)

Sit and recite:

التَّحِيَّاتُ لِلّٰهِ وَالصَّلَوَاتُ وَالطَّيِّبَاتُ، السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكَ أَيُّهَا النَّبِيُّ وَرَحْمَةُ اللّٰهِ وَبَرَكَاتُهُ، السَّلَامُ عَلَيْنَا وَعَلَىٰ عِبَادِ اللّٰهِ الصَّالِحِينَ، أَشْهَدُ أَنْ لَا إِلٰهَ إِلَّا اللّٰهُ وَأَشْهَدُ أَنَّ مُحَمَّدًا عَبْدُهُ وَرَسُولُهُ

English:
At-tahiyyatu lillahi was-salawatu wat-tayyibat, Assalamu ‘alaika ayyuhan-Nabiyyu wa rahmatullahi wa barakatuh, Assalamu ‘alaina wa ‘ala ‘ibadillahis-salihin, Ash-hadu an la ilaha illallah, wa ash-hadu anna Muhammadan ‘abduhu wa rasuluh

Hindi:
अत-तहिय्यातु लिल्लाही वस-सलवातु वत-तय्यिबात, अस्सलामु अलैका अय्युहन-नबिय्यु व रहमतुल्लाही व बरकातुह, अस्सलामु अलैना व अला इबादिल्लाहिस-सालिहीन, अश-हदु अन ला इलाहा इल्लल्लाह, व अश-हदु अन्न मुहम्मदन अब्दुहु व रसूलुह

Translation:
All greetings, prayers, and good things are for Allah. Peace be upon you, O Prophet, and the mercy and blessings of Allah. Peace be upon us and upon the righteous servants of Allah. I bear witness that there is no god but Allah, and I bear witness that Muhammad is His servant and Messenger.

11. Salat Ibrahimiyyah (Durood)

Recite:

اللّٰهُمَّ صَلِّ عَلَىٰ مُحَمَّدٍ وَعَلَىٰ آلِ مُحَمَّدٍ كَمَا صَلَّيْتَ عَلَىٰ إِبْرَاهِيمَ وَعَلَىٰ آلِ إِبْرَاهِيمَ إِنَّكَ حَمِيدٌ مَجِيدٌ، اللّٰهُمَّ بَارِكْ عَلَىٰ مُحَمَّدٍ وَعَلَىٰ آلِ مُحَمَّدٍ كَمَا بَارَكْتَ عَلَىٰ إِبْرَاهِيمَ وَعَلَىٰ آلِ إِبْرَاهِيمَ إِنَّكَ حَمِيدٌ مَجِيدٌ

English:
Allahumma salli ‘ala Muhammadin wa ‘ala ali Muhammadin kama sallayta ‘ala Ibrahima wa ‘ala ali Ibrahima innaka Hamidun Majid. Allahumma barik ‘ala Muhammadin wa ‘ala ali Muhammadin kama barakta ‘ala Ibrahima wa ‘ala ali Ibrahima innaka Hamidun Majid.

Hindi:
अल्लाहुम्मा सल्ली अला मुहम्मदिन व अला आलि मुहम्मदिन कामा सल्लयता अला इब्राहीमा व अला आलि इब्राहीमा इन्नका हमीदुन मजीद। अल्लाहुम्मा बारिक अला मुहम्मदिन व अला आलि मुहम्मदिन कामा बारकता अला इब्राहीमा व अला आलि इब्राहीमा इन्नका हमीदुन मजीद।

Translation:
O Allah, send blessings upon Muhammad and upon the family of Muhammad as You sent blessings upon Ibrahim and upon the family of Ibrahim; indeed, You are Praiseworthy, Glorious. O Allah, bless Muhammad and the family of Muhammad as You blessed Ibrahim and the family of Ibrahim; indeed, You are Praiseworthy, Glorious.

12. Dua After Durood

Recite a short dua, such as:

رَبَّنَا آتِنَا فِي الدُّنْيَا حَسَنَةً وَفِي الْآخِرَةِ حَسَنَةً وَقِنَا عَذَابَ النَّارِ

English: Rabbana atina fid-dunya hasanatan wa fil-akhirati hasanatan wa qina ‘adhaban-nar

Hindi: रब्बना आतिना फिद-दुन्या हसनतन व फिल-आखिरति हसनतन व किना अज़ाबन-नार

Translation: Our Lord, give us good in this world and good in the Hereafter, and protect us from the punishment of the Fire.

13. Salam (Ending the Prayer)

Turn your head to the right and left, saying:

السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُمْ وَرَحْمَةُ اللّٰهِ

English: Assalamu ‘alaikum wa rahmatullah

Hindi: अस्सलामु अलैकुम व रहमतुल्लाह

Translation: Peace and mercy of Allah be upon you.

Second Set: Two Rak’ahs

Repeat the steps above for another two Rak’ahs with the same intention.

Fard Rak’ahs (4 Rak’ahs)

Note: The Fard Rak’ahs are prayed in one set of four Rak’ahs. When alone, all recitations are silent. In congregation, the Imam recites Surah Al-Fatiha and another Surah aloud in the first two Rak’ahs, and the last two are silent.

نَوَيْتُ أَنْ أُصَلِّيَ لِلّٰهِ تَعَالَىٰ أَرْبَعَ رَكَعَاتٍ صَلَاةِ الظُّهْرِ فَرْضًا

English: Nawaytu an usalli lillahi ta’ala arba’a raka’atin salatidh dhuhr fardan

Hindi: नवायतु अन उस्सल्ली लिल्लाही तआला अरबआ रकआतिन सलातिझ झुहर फर्दन

Translation: I intend to offer four Rak’ahs of Dhuhr Fard prayer for Allah, the Exalted.

First and Second Rak’ahs

Follow the same steps as the Sunnah Rak’ahs (steps 1-13). Perform Tashahhud after the second Rak’ah but do not recite Salat Ibrahimiyyah or end with Salam. Stand up for the third Rak’ah.

Third and Fourth Rak’ahs

Recite only Surah Al-Fatiha in Qiyam (step 3) for both Rak’ahs, then proceed with steps 4-8. After the fourth Rak’ah, perform Tashahhud, Salat Ibrahimiyyah, Dua, and Salam (steps 10-13).

Final Sunnah Rak’ahs (2 Rak’ahs)

Note: The final two Sunnah Rak’ahs are optional but recommended, prayed like the first Sunnah set with the same intention.

Repeat the steps for the first Sunnah set (steps 1-13).

Tips for Perfecting Dhuhr Salah

Dhuhr Salah can be challenging due to midday commitments, but its rewards are immense. Here are some tips:

  • Scheduling: Plan a fixed time and place, such as a quiet corner at work or home.
  • Spiritual Motivation: Reflect on the Hadith: "The one who prays Dhuhr will have their sins forgiven as if they were a newborn" (Sahih Bukhari).
  • Avoid Mistakes: Don’t rush recitations; use the transliterations for correct pronunciation.
  • After Dhuhr: Recite Ayatul Kursi and Dhikr for added blessings.

Consistency in Dhuhr strengthens your connection with Allah. Seek His help to make it a daily habit.

Conclusion

Dhuhr Salah is a vital pause in your day to reconnect with Allah (SWT). With four Rak’ahs Sunnah Mu’akkadah, four Fard, and two Sunnah, it’s a comprehensive act of worship.
