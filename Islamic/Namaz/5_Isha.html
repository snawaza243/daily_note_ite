A Complete Guide to Isha Salah: Step-by-Step with Arabic, English, and Hindi Transliteration

Introduction

Isha Salah, the night prayer, is the fifth and final daily prayer in Islam, performed after twilight until midnight. It’s a time to seek forgiveness and prepare for the night’s rest under Allah’s protection. The Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) said, "Whoever prays Isha in congregation is as if he has prayed half the night" (Sahih Muslim). This guide details the four Rak’ahs of Fard, two Rak’ahs of Sunnah Mu’akkadah, and the Witr prayer (typically three Rak’ahs) for Isha Salah, including Arabic recitations, their transliterations in English and Hindi, and translations. Whether you’re learning to pray or refining your Salah, this blog will help you perform Isha with devotion and ease.

Understanding Isha Salah

Isha Salah is performed after the redness of twilight disappears until midnight (or before Fajr). It consists of four Rak’ahs Fard (obligatory), two Rak’ahs Sunnah Mu’akkadah (highly recommended), and the Witr prayer (obligatory in Hanafi school, typically three Rak’ahs). The Quran emphasizes night prayers: "Establish prayer at the decline of the sun until the darkness of the night" (Surah Al-Isra, 17:78). In congregation, the Fard Rak’ahs are recited aloud by the Imam in the first two Rak’ahs; when alone, they are also recited aloud. The Sunnah and Witr are prayed silently and individually. To prepare, perform Wudu, face the Qibla, and set a clear intention. To maintain consistency, pray Isha early to avoid fatigue.

Step-by-Step Guide to Isha Salah

Isha Salah consists of four Rak’ahs Fard, two Rak’ahs Sunnah Mu’akkadah, and three Rak’ahs Witr. Below is the detailed process for each Rak’ah, with recitations in Arabic, English transliteration, Hindi transliteration, and translations.

Fard Rak’ahs (4 Rak’ahs)

Note: The Fard Rak’ahs are prayed in one set of four Rak’ahs. The first two Rak’ahs are recited aloud (by the Imam in congregation or alone), while the last two are recited silently.

First Rak’ah

1. Niyyah (Intention)

Make a silent intention in your heart for four Rak’ahs of Isha Fard.

نَوَيْتُ أَنْ أُصَلِّيَ لِلّٰهِ تَعَالَىٰ أَرْبَعَ رَكَعَاتٍ صَلَاةِ الْعِشَاءِ فَرْضًا

English: Nawaytu an usalli lillahi ta’ala arba’a raka’atin salatil isha fardan

Hindi: नवायतु अन उस्सल्ली लिल्लाही तआला अरबआ रकआतिन सलातिल इशा फर्दन

Translation: I intend to offer four Rak’ahs of Isha Fard prayer for Allah, the Exalted.

2. Takbiratul Ihram (Opening Takbir)

Raise your hands to ear level, say “Allahu Akbar,” and begin the prayer.

اللّٰهُ أَكْبَرُ

English: Allahu Akbar

Hindi: अल्लाहु अकबर

Translation: Allah is the Greatest.

3. Qiyam (Standing) - Recite Surah Al-Fatiha

Recite Surah Al-Fatiha aloud (or silently if following an Imam), followed by a short Surah (e.g., Surah Al-Ikhlas).

بِسْمِ ٱللَّهِ ٱلرَّحْمَٰنِ ٱلرَّحِيمِ
ٱلْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ رَبِّ ٱلْعَٰلَمِينَ
ٱلرَّحْمَٰنِ ٱلرَّحِيمِ
مَٰلِكِ يَوْمِ ٱلدِّينِ
إِيَّاكَ نَعْبُدُ وَإِيَّاكَ نَسْتَعِينُ
ٱهْدِنَا ٱلصِّرَٰطَ ٱلْمُسْتَقِيمَ
صِرَٰطَ ٱلَّذِينَ أَنْعَمْتَ عَلَيْهِمْ غَيْرِ ٱلْمَغْضُوبِ عَلَيْهِمْ وَلَا ٱلضَّآلِّينَ

English:
Bismillahir Rahmanir Rahim
Alhamdu lillahi Rabbil ‘alamin
Ar-Rahmanir Rahim
Maliki yawmiddin
Iyyaka na’budu wa iyyaka nasta’in
Ihdinas siratal mustaqim
Siratal ladhina an’amta ‘alayhim ghayril magdubi ‘alayhim walad dallin

Hindi:
बिस्मिल्लाहिर रहमानिर रहीम
अलहमदु लिल्लाही रब्बिल आलमीन
अर-रहमानिर रहीम
मालिकी यौमिद्दीन
इय्या नअबुदु व इय्या नस्तईन
इहदिनस सिरातल मुस्तकीम
सिरातल लज़ीना अनअमता अलयहिम गैरिल मगदूबि अलयहिम वलद दाल्लीन

Translation:
In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful.
All praise is due to Allah, Lord of all the worlds.
The Most Gracious, the Most Merciful.
Master of the Day of Judgment.
You alone we worship, and You alone we ask for help.
Guide us to the straight path,
The path of those upon whom You have bestowed favor, not of those who have earned Your anger, nor of those who are astray.

4. Ruku (Bowing)

Bow and say “Subhana Rabbiyal Adheem” three times.

سُبْحَانَ رَبِّيَ الْعَظِيمِ

English: Subhana Rabbiyal Adheem

Hindi: सुभान रब्बियल अज़ीम

Translation: Glory be to my Lord, the Most Great.

5. Qawmah (Standing after Ruku)

Stand up and say:

سَمِعَ اللّٰهُ لِمَنْ حَمِدَهُ، رَبَّنَا وَلَكَ الْحَمْدُ

English: Sami’Allahu liman hamidah, Rabbana wa lakal hamd

Hindi: समिअल्लाहु लिमन हमिदह, रब्बना व लकल हम्द

Translation: Allah hears those who praise Him; Our Lord, to You be all praise.

6. Sujood (Prostration)

Prostrate and say “Subhana Rabbiyal A’la” three times.

سُبْحَانَ رَبِّيَ الْأَعْلَىٰ

English: Subhana Rabbiyal A’la

Hindi: सुभान रब्बियल आला

Translation: Glory be to my Lord, the Most High.

7. Jalsa (Sitting between Prostrations)

Sit briefly and say:

رَبِّ اغْفِرْ لِي

English: Rabbighfir li

Hindi: रब्बिगफिर ली

Translation: My Lord, forgive me.

8. Second Sujood

Repeat the prostration and say “Subhana Rabbiyal A’la” three times (same as step 6).

Second Rak’ah

9. Qiyam (Standing)

Stand up and repeat steps 3-8 (recite Surah Al-Fatiha and another Surah aloud, then perform Ruku, Qawmah, Sujood, Jalsa, and second Sujood).

10. Tashahhud (Sitting)

Sit and recite:

التَّحِيَّاتُ لِلّٰهِ وَالصَّلَوَاتُ وَالطَّيِّبَاتُ، السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكَ أَيُّهَا النَّبِيُّ وَرَحْمَةُ اللّٰهِ وَبَرَكَاتُهُ، السَّلَامُ عَلَيْنَا وَعَلَىٰ عِبَادِ اللّٰهِ الصَّالِحِينَ، أَشْهَدُ أَنْ لَا إِلٰهَ إِلَّا اللّٰهُ وَأَشْهَدُ أَنَّ مُحَمَّدًا عَبْدُهُ وَرَسُولُهُ

English:
At-tahiyyatu lillahi was-salawatu wat-tayyibat, Assalamu ‘alaika ayyuhan-Nabiyyu wa rahmatullahi wa barakatuh, Assalamu ‘alaina wa ‘ala ‘ibadillahis-salihin, Ash-hadu an la ilaha illallah, wa ash-hadu anna Muhammadan ‘abduhu wa rasuluh

Hindi:
अत-تहिय्यातु लिल्लाही वस-सलवातु वत-तय्यिबात, अस्सलामु अलैका अय्युहन-नबिय्यु व रहमतुल्लाही व बरकातुह, अस्सलामु अलैना व अला इबादिल्लाहिस-सालिहीन, अश-हदु अन ला इलाहा इल्लल्लाह, व अश-हदु अन्न मुहम्मदन अब्दुहु व रसूलुह

Translation:
All greetings, prayers, and good things are for Allah. Peace be upon you, O Prophet, and the mercy and blessings of Allah. Peace be upon us and upon the righteous servants of Allah. I bear witness that there is no god but Allah, and I bear witness that Muhammad is His servant and Messenger.

Third and Fourth Rak’ahs

11. Qiyam (Standing)

Stand up and recite only Surah Al-Fatiha silently for both Rak’ahs, then repeat steps 4-8 (Ruku, Qawmah, Sujood, Jalsa, and second Sujood).

12. Tashahhud (Sitting)

After the fourth Rak’ah, sit and recite the Tashahhud (same as step 10).

13. Salat Ibrahimiyyah (Durood)

Recite:

اللّٰهُمَّ صَلِّ عَلَىٰ مُحَمَّدٍ وَعَلَىٰ آلِ مُحَمَّدٍ كَمَا صَلَّيْتَ عَلَىٰ إِبْرَاهِيمَ وَعَلَىٰ آلِ إِبْرَاهِيمَ إِنَّكَ حَمِيدٌ مَجِيدٌ، اللّٰهُمَّ بَارِكْ عَلَىٰ مُحَمَّدٍ وَعَلَىٰ آلِ مُحَمَّدٍ كَمَا بَارَكْتَ عَلَىٰ إِبْرَاهِيمَ وَعَلَىٰ آلِ إِبْرَاهِيمَ إِنَّكَ حَمِيدٌ مَجِيدٌ

English:
Allahumma salli ‘ala Muhammadin wa ‘ala ali Muhammadin kama sallayta ‘ala Ibrahima wa ‘ala ali Ibrahima innaka Hamidun Majid. Allahumma barik ‘ala Muhammadin wa ‘ala ali Muhammadin kama barakta ‘ala Ibrahima wa ‘ala ali Ibrahima innaka Hamidun Majid.

Hindi:
अल्लाहुम्मा सल्ली अला मुहम्मदिन व अला आलि मुहम्मदिन कामा सल्लयता अला इब्राहीमा व अला आलि इब्राहीमा इन्नका हमीदुन मजीद। अल्लाहुम्मा बारिक अला मुहम्मदिन व अला आलि मुहम्मदिन कामा बारकता अला इब्राहीमा व अला आलि इब्राहीमा इन्नका हमीदुन मजीद।

Translation:
O Allah, send blessings upon Muhammad and upon the family of Muhammad as You sent blessings upon Ibrahim and upon the family of Ibrahim; indeed, You are Praiseworthy, Glorious. O Allah, bless Muhammad and the family of Muhammad as You blessed Ibrahim and the family of Ibrahim; indeed, You are Praiseworthy, Glorious.

14. Dua After Durood

Recite a short dua, such as:

رَبَّنَا آتِنَا فِي الدُّنْيَا حَسَنَةً وَفِي الْآخِرَةِ حَسَنَةً وَقِنَا عَذَابَ النَّارِ

English: Rabbana atina fid-dunya hasanatan wa fil-akhirati hasanatan wa qina ‘adhaban-nar

Hindi: रब्बना आतिना फिद-दुन्या हसनतन व फिल-आखिरति हसनतन व किना अज़ाबन-नार

Translation: Our Lord, give us good in this world and good in the Hereafter, and protect us from the punishment of the Fire.

15. Salam (Ending the Prayer)

Turn your head to the right and left, saying:

السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُمْ وَرَحْمَةُ اللّٰهِ

English: Assalamu ‘alaikum wa rahmatullah

Hindi: अस्सलामु अलैकुम व रहमतुल्लाह

Translation: Peace and mercy of Allah be upon you.

Sunnah Mu’akkadah Rak’ahs (2 Rak’ahs)

Note: The Sunnah Mu’akkadah Rak’ahs are highly recommended, prayed individually and silently, similar to Fajr and Maghrib Sunnah.

نَوَيْتُ أَنْ أُصَلِّيَ لِلّٰهِ تَعَالَىٰ رَكْعَتَيْ صَلَاةِ الْعِشَاءِ سُنَّةً مُؤَكَّدَةً

English: Nawaytu an usalli lillahi ta’ala rak’atay salatil isha sunnatan mu’akkadatan

Hindi: नवायतु अन उस्सल्ली लिल्लाही तआला रकअतय सलातिल इशा सुन्नतन मुआक्कदतन

Translation: I intend to offer two Rak’ahs of Isha Sunnah Mu’akkadah prayer for Allah, the Exalted.

First and Second Rak’ahs

Follow the same steps as the Fard Rak’ahs (steps 1-10 for the first two Rak’ahs, but recited silently), then perform Tashahhud, Salat Ibrahimiyyah, Dua, and Salam (steps 12-15).

Witr Prayer (3 Rak’ahs)

Note: The Witr prayer is obligatory in the Hanafi school and highly recommended in others, typically prayed as three Rak’ahs with one Salam at the end, recited silently and individually.

نَوَيْتُ أَنْ أُصَلِّيَ لِلّٰهِ تَعَالَىٰ ثَلَاثَ رَكَعَاتٍ صَلَاةِ الْوِتْرِ

English: Nawaytu an usalli lillahi ta’ala thalatha raka’atin salatil witr

Hindi: नवायतु अन उस्सल्ली लिल्लाही तआला थलाथ रकआतिन सलातिल वित्र

Translation: I intend to offer three Rak’ahs of Witr prayer for Allah, the Exalted.

First and Second Rak’ahs

Follow the same steps as the Sunnah Rak’ahs (steps 1-10, recited silently), including Surah Al-Fatiha and another Surah in Qiyam. After the second Rak’ah, perform Tashahhud (step 10) but do not end the prayer.

Third Rak’ah

16. Qiyam (Standing)

Stand up, recite Surah Al-Fatiha and a short Surah silently, then say “Allahu Akbar” and raise your hands for Dua Qunoot.

17. Dua Qunoot

Recite Dua Qunoot with hands raised (Hanafi school: hands folded as in Qiyam):

اللّٰهُمَّ إِنَّا نَسْتَعِينُكَ وَنَسْتَغْفِرُكَ وَنُؤْمِنُ بِكَ وَنَتَوَكَّلُ عَلَيْكَ وَنُثْنِي عَلَيْكَ الْخَيْرَ، وَنَشْكُرُكَ وَلَا نَكْفُرُكَ، وَنَخْلَعُ وَنَتْرُكُ مَنْ يَفْجُرُكَ، اللّٰهُمَّ إِيَّاكَ نَعْبُدُ، وَلَكَ نُصَلِّي وَنَسْجُدُ، وَإِلَيْكَ نَسْعَىٰ وَنَحْفِدُ، نَرْجُو رَحْمَتَكَ، وَنَخْشَىٰ عَذَابَكَ، إِنَّ عَذَابَكَ الْجِدَّ بِالْكُفَّارِ مُلْحَقٌ

English:
Allahumma inna nasta’inuka wa nastaghfiruk, wa nu’minu bik, wa natawakkalu ‘alayk, wa nuthni ‘alaykal khayr, wa nashkuruk wa la nakfuruk, wa nakhla’u wa natruku man yafjuruk. Allahumma iyyaka na’budu, wa laka nusalli wa nasjud, wa ilayka nas’a wa nahfid, narju rahmatak, wa nakhsha ‘adhabak, inna ‘adhabakal jidda bil kuffari mulhaq.

Hindi:
अल्लाहुम्मा इन्ना नस्तईनुका व नस्तगफिरुक, व नुअमिनु बिक, व नतवक्कलु अलयक, व नुस्नी अलयकल खयर, व नश्कुरुक व ला नकफुरुक, व नख्लउ व नत्रु쿠 मन यफ्जुरुक। अल्लाहुम्मा इय्याक नअबुदु, व लक नुसल्ली व नस्जुद, व इलयक नसआ व नहफिद, नरजू रहमतक, व नख्शा अज़ाबक, इन्न अज़ाबकल जिद्द बिल कुफ्फारि मुलहक।

Translation:
O Allah, we seek Your help and ask for Your forgiveness, believe in You, rely on You, and praise You with all good. We thank You and are not ungrateful to You, and we disown and abandon those who disobey You. O Allah, You alone we worship, to You we pray and prostrate, and toward You we strive and hasten. We hope for Your mercy and fear Your punishment. Indeed, Your severe punishment overtakes the disbelievers.

18. Complete the Rak’ah

After Dua Qunoot, perform Ruku, Qawmah, Sujood, Jalsa, and second Sujood (steps 4-8). Then sit for Tashahhud, Salat Ibrahimiyyah, Dua, and Salam (steps 12-15).

Tips for Perfecting Isha Salah

Isha Salah can be challenging due to nighttime fatigue, but its rewards are immense. Here are some tips:

  • Timeliness: Pray Isha early to avoid drowsiness, ideally after Maghrib or dinner.
  • Spiritual Motivation: Reflect on the Hadith: "The one who prays Isha will have their night blessed" (Sunan Tirmidhi).
  • Avoid Mistakes: Memorize Dua Qunoot using the transliterations; recite clearly and avoid rushing.
  • After Isha: Perform Witr promptly and recite evening Adhkar or Ayatul Kursi for protection.

Consistency in Isha and Witr brings tranquility. Seek Allah’s help to make it a daily habit.

Conclusion

Isha Salah, with its four Fard Rak’ahs, two Sunnah Mu’akkadah, and three Witr Rak’ahs, is a powerful way to end your day in worship.
