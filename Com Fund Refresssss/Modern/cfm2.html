How Computers Handle Data

Analog Computers

Analog computers measure continuous physical quantities like voltage, speed, or temperature. They're like mercury thermometers - the level changes smoothly with temperature.

Modern Use: Still used in specialized applications like flight simulators and medical monitoring equipment.

Hybrid Computers

Hybrid computers combine analog and digital components. NASA uses them for space mission simulations where real-time sensor data (analog) needs digital processing.

Did You Know? Your smartphone is about 1,000 times faster than the computer that landed Apollo 11 on the moon! Modern microcomputers pack incredible power into tiny form factors.

Microcomputers are single-user systems with a microprocessor as their CPU. They range from desktop PCs to embedded systems in smart refrigerators and IoT devices. Tesla's car computers are advanced microcomputers that process sensor data in real-time.

Mainframes are powerful computers used by large organizations for critical applications like banking transactions. They can handle thousands of users simultaneously with high reliability. Modern mainframes like IBM z16 can process 19 billion transactions per day.
